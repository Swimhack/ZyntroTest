1. Industry Problem Analysis

Current State of Peptide Payment Processing

Industry Overview:

  • Research peptide market: $500M-1B annually (US)
  • 200-500 active online peptide vendors
  • Average monthly sales per merchant: $50k-200k
  • Primary sales channel: Direct-to-consumer e-commerce

Payment Processing Challenges:

  1. High Decline Rates
    • 30-50% of transactions declined by traditional processors
    • Average merchant loses $100k-500k annually in declined sales
  2. Account Termination Risk
    • 60-70% of peptide merchants experience account closure within 12 months
    • No advance notice in most cases
    • Funds often held 180+ days after termination
  3. Excessive Fees
    • Traditional rates: 2.9% + $0.30
    • High-risk rates: 4-8% + $0.50
    • Reserve requirements: 5-15% rolling reserve
    • Chargeback fees: $25-100 per incident
  4. Chargeback Issues
    • Industry average: 2-5% (vs 0.5-1% for general e-commerce)
    • Primary reasons: Product quality disputes, "Research purposes only" confusion, Delayed shipping, Customer remorse after health concerns

2. Why Payment Processors Flag Peptides

Visa/Mastercard Risk Factors

Card Network Classification: Peptides categorized as "high-risk" or "prohibited" depending on claims

Automatic Flagging Triggers:

  1. Keyword Detection
    • "Peptide," "research chemicals," "not for human consumption"
    • "HGH," "IGF-1," "TB-500," specific peptide names
    • Medical claims or health benefits
    • Anti-aging, muscle growth, fat loss terminology
  2. Website Content Analysis
    • Merchant websites scanned for restricted products
    • Disclaimer language triggers review
    • Product catalogs analyzed for banned substances
  3. Transaction Patterns
    • Recurring international orders
    • High average order values ($200-500)
    • Spike in chargebacks

3. Legal Risks of Non-Compliance

⚠️ Critical: Criminal Liability

Transaction Laundering (18 USC § 1956-1957)

Definition: Processing payments through a merchant account obtained through false pretenses or misrepresentation of the business type.

Examples:

  • Using "vitamin supplement" MCC for peptide sales
  • Misrepresenting business description to payment processor
  • Routing transactions through shell companies
  • Manipulating transaction descriptors to avoid detection

Penalties:

  • Up to 20 years federal prison
  • Fines up to $500,000 per violation
  • Forfeiture of proceeds
  • Restitution to payment processors

Wire Fraud (18 USC § 1343)

Penalties:

  • Up to 20 years federal prison
  • Fines up to $250,000 per count
  • Multiple counts for each transaction

Civil Liability

MATCH List Consequences:

  • Permanent or 5-year ban from obtaining merchant accounts
  • Shared across all major processors globally
  • Extends to business owners personally (not just company)
  • Extremely difficult to remove even after compliance

4. Legitimate Solution Options

Option 1: Compliant High-Risk Merchant Services

Recommended Approach: Build a payment gateway that works WITH (not against) card networks by partnering with legitimate high-risk payment processors.

How It Works:

  • Use proper high-risk MCCs (5912, 5047, or custom negotiated codes)
  • Enhanced fraud detection (AI-powered)
  • Real-time transaction monitoring
  • Automated chargeback management

Pricing Structure:

Component Merchant Cost Your Revenue
Transaction Fee 3.5-4.5% + $0.30 1.0-1.5%
Platform Fee $299-499/month $299-499/month (100%)
Chargeback Protection $1.00 per transaction Varies

Partner Payment Processors:

  1. Durango Merchant Services - 90% approval rate for compliant merchants
  2. PaymentCloud - Fast approval (24-48 hours)
  3. SMB Global - International processing available
  4. eMerchantBroker - CBD/supplement experience

Pros:

  • ✅ Legal and sustainable
  • ✅ Scalable (residual income model)
  • ✅ Value-added services (compliance consulting)
  • ✅ Lower merchant acquisition cost

Cons:

  • ❌ Higher fees required (3.5-4.5% vs 2.9%)
  • ❌ Strict compliance requirements
  • ❌ Reserve requirements (5-10% rolling)
  • ❌ Partnership dependence

Option 2: Cryptocurrency Payment Gateway

Key Benefit: Bypasses card networks entirely. Peptides are legal to sell; the issue is card network policies, not law.

How It Works:

  • WooCommerce crypto payment plugin
  • Automatic USD conversion via Coinbase Commerce
  • Supports BTC, ETH, USDC
  • No chargebacks (transactions irreversible)

Pricing:

  • 1.5% transaction fee (your cut)
  • $99/month platform subscription
  • Optional: 0.5% instant USD conversion fee

Pros:

  • ✅ No card network restrictions
  • ✅ Lower fees (1.5-2.5% vs 3.5-4.5%)
  • ✅ Global reach
  • ✅ Higher profit margins
  • ✅ Future-proof

Cons:

  • ❌ Limited customer adoption (15-20% own crypto)
  • ❌ Price volatility
  • ❌ No consumer protection (irreversible payments)
  • ❌ Regulatory uncertainty
  • ❌ Technical complexity

Option 3: ACH/Bank Transfer + Subscription Model

How It Works:

  1. Customers pay monthly subscription for "access" ($29-99/month)
  2. Actual purchases via ACH bank transfer
  3. 0.5-1% ACH fee (vs 3% card fee)
  4. 3-5 day settlement

Pricing:

  • $499/month platform fee
  • 0.8% ACH transaction fee
  • $99 setup fee

Pros:

  • ✅ No card network involvement
  • ✅ Lowest transaction fees (0.5-0.8%)
  • ✅ Recurring revenue from subscriptions
  • ✅ Reduced fraud
  • ✅ Customer lock-in

Cons:

  • ❌ Customer friction (two-step process)
  • ❌ Subscription fatigue
  • ❌ ACH limitations (transaction limits)
  • ❌ 3-5 day settlement delay
